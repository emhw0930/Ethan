Feng Chia University:

Computer Vision Summer Intern: link
This was my first summer internship, located in Taichung, Taiwan. As a computer vision intern, I learned how to use YOLO V5, OpenCV, and Python to train a customized dataset of Sambar Deer images for object detection. This enabled me to detect the position and activity of the deer in the frame for the local animal farm.

The most challenging obstacle I faced during the internship was ensuring that the index for each deer remained consistent throughout the entire video. Since the deer would move around and the index provided by YOLO V5 differed in every frame, I calculated the Euclidean distance between each deer in the current frame and every other deer in the previous frame. Then, I assigned the index of the current frame's deer to be the index of the closest deer from the previous frame based on this distance calculation. This approach ensured that the index of the same deer remained constant throughout the entire video.

Throughout this internship, I learned the basic of computer vision, including how to train a dataset, label image, use OpenCV, and fine tune the model.
