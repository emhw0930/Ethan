Background:

I currently reside in Berkeley, California, but I was born and raised in Taipei, Taiwan. I love to eat Japanese, Korean, and Taiwanese food, and I enjoy playing badminton, basketball, and table tennis during my spare time. Most importantly, I love to code.

My first experience with coding was during my high school years when I participated in VEX Robotics. Our goal was to build a robot that could move and perform tasks autonomously without the need for manual control, and this is where coding came into play. After my experience in robotics, I discovered my interest in the field of computer science, where coding can be used to achieve great things!

Upon entering college, I enrolled in a class called CS88, Computational Structure in Data Science, and this is where I began my computer science journey. Due to the excellent content, pace, and resources of this course, I confirmed my desire to major in computer science and became eager to be part of the course staff.

After taking CS88 during my first semester of sophomore year, I was certain that I wanted to major in computer science. Therefore, I decided to take CS61A, CS61B, and CS70 during the second semester of my sophomore year. During that semester, I also joined the CS88 Course Staff as an academic intern.

Computer Scinece + Teaching?

As an academic intern in CS88, I learned how to conduct office hours, specifically by providing students with hints on homework, lab, and project questions without hindering their ability to learn.

Initially, as an international student whose native language isn't English, it was challenging for me to explain a question or concept effectively. However, after working as an Academic Intern for a semester, I learned how to improve my explanation skills and also enhanced my English speaking ability. Most importantly, I found great joy in teaching. Witnessing students grow and learn after I teach a concept is incredibly fulfilling for me, which is why I embarked on my journey in computer science teaching.

After being an academic intern, one of my life goals is to officially be a part of the course staff, eg tutor or TA. To accomplish this goal, I joined a club call Computer Science Mentor where we learn and grow as a teacher as well as getting know with other CS majors students. After applying and interviewing, I joined CS88 CSM as a junior mentor where I host weekly review sections for 5 students enrolled in CS88.
